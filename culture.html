Culture of Sri Lanka

The culture of Sri Lanka cannot be easily explained. Because it has got such a lot of interesting stuff. Primarily, the culture of Sri Lanka is based on Buddhism and Hinduism. Ancient Sri Lanka is well known for its excelled architecture styles, art of drawing and water management. Sri Lankan culture is very unique and thereby it contributes to the identity as well.

The culture of Sri Lanka includes Festivals, Arts, Crafts, Cuisine and Medicine.

Sinhala New Year

The biggest celebration in Sri Lanka that features a load of rituals and customs is the Sinhala & Tamil New Year where Sri Lankans are used to celebrate their new year every April.


Vesak Festival

The festival commemorates the birth, enlightenment (Nibbana), and passing
(Parinirvana) of Lord Buddha.




Art & Crafts

Masks, handloom, Dumbara Weaves, Batik and Laksha work depict the colourful and creative arts and crafts unique to Sri Lanka.



The Kandyan dance is highly sophisticated and refined. It flourished under the Kandyan kings from the 16th through the 19th centuries, and today it is considered the national dance of Sri Lanka.




Cuisine

The most vital part (for all the food lovers) that dominates the Sri Lankan culture is the mouth-watering food. The natural spices used are playing a special role in those food.











Ayurvedic medicine

Ayurvedic medicine is also a huge part of the culture which even has said to be able to cure terminal diseases such as cancer.
